Kapil Wankhede

Social Entrepreneur  ·  Institution Builder  ·  Education Leader

Wardha, Maharashtra


I grew up in Wardha, Maharashtra, as the child of daily-wage construction labourers. My parents' sacrifice enabled my education — a BTech in Engineering, then an MS in Finance at Illinois Institute of Technology, Chicago, and professional experience as a Quantitative Financial Analyst at Mitsubishi UFJ Financial Group in New York. What I observed there stayed with me: talent is widely distributed across India's communities, but access to opportunity is not.

That gap drove me to return to India in 2018 and rejoined — a grassroots educational movement supporting students from rural, tribal, nomadic, and migrant communities through mentorship, community libraries, digital learning, and residential education.

I teach mathematics and mentor students preparing for higher education and competitive exams. I am heading the Nalanda Abhiyan Campus development at Waifad — a 9.15-acre residential learning ecosystem targeting an October 2026 launch, with STEM labs, hostels, and a 120kW rooftop solar system. Through Nalanda AI Labs, I build AI and data tools to make quality education accessible in rural Maharashtra, including a 12-chapter Marathi-language Generative AI book for school students.

My technical work spans machine learning, NLP, data science, and applied AI — from disaster-response classifiers to transformer health monitoring systems. I am always exploring how emerging technology can drive equitable outcomes in education and society.
